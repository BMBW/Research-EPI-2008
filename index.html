Environmental Performance Index

What is Environmental Performance Index

In order to understand how a country performs towards the protection and the development of the environmental status and to take necessary actions to improve environmental sustainability some kind of Performance Index is needed. To fulfill those requirements EPI (Environmental Performance Index) has been introduced by Yale University (Yale Center for Environmental Law and Policy) Columbia University (Center for International Earth Science Information Network) in collaboration with the World Economic Forum and the Joint Research Centre of the European Commission. The EPI is a method of quantifying and numerically making the environmental performance of a state's policies. This index was developed from the Pilot Environmental Performance Index, first published in 2002, and designed to supplement the environmental targets set forth in the United Nations Millenium Development Goals.The EPI gives decisionmakers access to important environmental data organized in a way that is easily understandable, useful and drives productive competition.The EPI allows countries to compare their performance to neighbors and peers. The EPI focuses on two overarching environmental objectives:

  1. Reducing environmental stresses to human health (The Environmental Health objective);
  2. Promoting ecosystem vitality and sound natural resource management (the Ecosystem Vitality objective).

These broad goals also reflect the policy priorities of environmental authorities around the world and the international community's in adopting Goal 7 of the Millennium Development Goals (MDGs), to "ensure environmental sustainability." The two overarching objectives are gauged using several performance indicators tracked in several well-established policy categories, (These indicators and policy categories change from year to year) which are then combined to create a final EPI score. According to those EPI Scores chosen countries have been ranked to get a clear understanding about the Environmental Performance of each country and the world. In January 2012 four EPI reports have been released. The pilot 2006 Environmental Performance Index, and the 2008, 2010, and 2012 Environmental Performance Index. After 2012 report, EPI reports have been released once in every 2 years. That means 2014, 2016 and 2018 Environmental Performance Index.

This website focuses on showing the EPI scores of the countries in the world according to the EPI reports released in 2008, 2010 & 2012.
